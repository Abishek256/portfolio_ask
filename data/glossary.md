## XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return)

Definition: A method used to calculate returns on investments with irregular cash flows. It gives an annualized return percentage by considering the timing of each investment and withdrawal.

Example: Used to measure returns in SIPs or staggered investments.

---

## CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

Definition: The average annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period, assuming profits are reinvested.

Example: If an investment grows from ₹1,00,000 to ₹1,50,000 in 3 years, CAGR shows the annual growth rate.

---

## LTCG (Long Term Capital Gains)

Definition: Profit earned from selling assets held for more than a specified period (typically over 1 year for equities in India), taxed at concessional rates.

---

## STCG (Short Term Capital Gains)

Definition: Profit earned from selling assets held for a short duration (less than 1 year for equities), usually taxed at higher rates than LTCG.

---

## STT (Securities Transaction Tax)

Definition: A tax levied on the purchase and sale of securities listed on Indian stock exchanges.

---

## NFO (New Fund Offer)

Definition: The first-time subscription offer for a new mutual fund scheme launched by an asset management company.

---

## SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

Definition: A method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds, helping in disciplined investing and rupee cost averaging.

---

## ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme)

Definition: A tax-saving mutual fund that invests primarily in equities and qualifies for deductions under Section 80C.

---

## NAV (Net Asset Value)

Definition: The per-unit value of a mutual fund, calculated as total assets minus liabilities divided by the number of units.

---

## AUM (Assets Under Management)

Definition: The total market value of investments managed by a mutual fund or financial institution.

---

## Gilt Fund

Definition: A type of debt mutual fund that invests in government securities, carrying low credit risk but sensitive to interest rate changes.

---

## Duration Risk

Definition: The risk that bond prices will fall due to rising interest rates; higher duration means higher sensitivity.

---

## Expense Ratio

Definition: The annual fee charged by a mutual fund as a percentage of assets under management.

---

## Exit Load

Definition: A fee charged when investors redeem mutual fund units before a specified holding period.

---

## Rebalancing

Definition: The process of adjusting a portfolio’s asset allocation to maintain a desired risk level or investment strategy.

---

## HNI (High Net-worth Individual)

Definition: An individual with a large amount of investable wealth, typically above a defined threshold.

---

## Portfolio Overlap

Definition: The extent to which multiple funds or investments hold the same underlying securities.

---

## Benchmark

Definition: A standard index (like Nifty 50) used to compare the performance of a portfolio or fund.

---

## Alpha

Definition: The excess return of an investment relative to its benchmark, indicating active performance.

---

## Beta

Definition: A measure of an investment’s volatility compared to the overall market; beta > 1 means more volatile.
