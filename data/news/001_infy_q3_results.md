# Infosys Q3 FY25 Results: Revenue Beat, Guidance Raised

**Date:** January 16, 2025
**Ticker:** INFY | **Sector:** Information Technology

Infosys reported a stronger-than-expected third quarter for FY25, with consolidated revenue
growing 7.6% year-on-year in constant currency terms to $4.94 billion. Net profit came in at
₹6,806 crore, up 11.4% compared to the same quarter last year.

The company raised its full-year revenue growth guidance to 4.5–5.0% in constant currency,
up from the earlier range of 3.75–4.5%. Management cited a recovery in discretionary IT
spending among its European banking clients and strong deal wins in the AI and cloud migration
segments as the primary drivers.

CEO Salil Parekh highlighted a large deal total value (TCV) of $2.5 billion for the quarter,
which includes several multi-year transformation engagements. The company's headcount declined
marginally to 317,240 employees as it continued to optimise its workforce structure.

Analysts at domestic brokerages revised their target prices upward, with the consensus now
sitting around ₹1,650–1,700. The stock responded positively on the day of results, gaining
approximately 4% in intraday trade on the NSE.

Key risk flagged by management: continued macro uncertainty in the US financial services
vertical and potential client budget freezes heading into calendar year 2025.
