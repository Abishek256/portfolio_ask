# Wipro Q3 FY25: Margins Under Pressure, Guidance Disappoints

**Date:** January 17, 2025
**Ticker:** WIPRO | **Sector:** Information Technology

Wipro's third quarter FY25 results came in below street expectations, with IT services revenue
declining 0.5% sequentially to $2.63 billion. The company's EBIT margin contracted by 40 basis
points to 16.1%, weaker than the analyst consensus of 16.5%.

For Q4 FY25, Wipro guided IT services revenue in the range of $2.57–2.62 billion, implying
flat to marginal growth — a figure that disappointed investors who had expected a recovery
signal. Management attributed the subdued outlook to continued weakness in the BFSI
(Banking, Financial Services & Insurance) vertical and delayed decision-making by clients
in the manufacturing sector.

CEO Srinivas Pallia acknowledged that deal ramp-ups from large contracts signed in early FY25
have been slower than anticipated. The company's large deal wins for Q3 stood at $1.5 billion
TCV, lower than Infosys and TCS reported for the same period.

Wipro's stock fell approximately 6% following the results announcement, underperforming
the broader IT index. Several analysts downgraded the stock citing poor visibility on revenue
recovery, with revised target prices ranging from ₹380 to ₹420.

This divergence between WIPRO and INFY in the same quarter illustrates the importance of
evaluating IT companies individually rather than as a monolithic sector bet.
