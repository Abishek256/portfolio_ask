# HDFC Bank: Merger Integration Progresses, Loan Growth Moderates Deliberately

**Date:** February 3, 2025
**Ticker:** HDFCBANK | **Sector:** Banking & Financial Services

HDFC Bank's management provided an update on its ongoing integration with erstwhile HDFC Ltd,
nearly 18 months after the landmark merger concluded. The bank's loan-to-deposit ratio (LDR),
which had spiked post-merger due to the inherited mortgage book, has been gradually normalising
toward the 85–87% range that management targets.

Net Interest Margin (NIM) for Q3 FY25 came in at 3.43%, slightly below the 3.5% reported
pre-merger, as the bank continues to reprice its higher-cost deposits and shed some
lower-yielding wholesale loans. Management reiterated that NIM compression is transient and
expects recovery toward 3.6–3.7% by end of FY26.

The bank reported gross NPA ratio of 1.42%, stable versus the prior quarter, indicating
no material deterioration in asset quality despite macro headwinds in the unsecured retail
lending segment. Deposits grew 15.8% year-on-year, outpacing the system — a key positive
for the LDR normalisation thesis.

Markets have been cautious on HDFCBANK over the past 12 months given the merger-related
overhangs. The stock has underperformed the Nifty Bank index by approximately 9% over the
last year. Analysts with a 12-18 month view remain constructive, citing the franchise strength
and improving fundamentals.
