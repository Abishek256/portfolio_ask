# ICICI Bank Q3 FY25: Retail Loans Surge, Asset Quality Holds Firm

**Date:** January 25, 2025
**Ticker:** ICICIBANK | **Sector:** Banking & Financial Services

ICICI Bank delivered a strong Q3 FY25, reporting net profit of ₹11,792 crore — a 15.1%
year-on-year increase — driven by robust retail loan growth and disciplined cost management.
The bank's retail loan book grew 18% year-on-year, led by home loans, vehicle loans, and
personal credit.

Net Interest Margin held steady at 4.25%, among the highest in the private banking sector,
reflecting the bank's ability to maintain pricing power even in a competitive deposit market.
The management attributed margin resilience to a favorable loan mix shift toward higher-yielding
retail products.

Gross NPA ratio improved to 2.06% from 2.30% in the year-ago quarter, reflecting strong
collections and disciplined underwriting standards. The provision coverage ratio stood at 79%,
providing a comfortable buffer against any future slippages.

ICICI Bank's stock has been one of the top performers in the Nifty Bank index over the last
12 months, gaining approximately 22%. Analysts widely view ICICI Bank as the highest-quality
private bank franchise in India, with several institutions holding it as a top pick in the
BFSI sector.

The contrast with HDFC Bank's ongoing integration challenges highlights that not all bank
stocks carry the same risk profile even within the same sector — a critical point for
sector-level portfolio analysis.
