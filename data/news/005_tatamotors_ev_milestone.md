# Tata Motors Crosses 1 Lakh EV Sales in FY25, JLR Profitability Strengthens

**Date:** March 12, 2025
**Ticker:** TATAMOTORS | **Sector:** Automobile

Tata Motors announced that its electric vehicle division crossed the cumulative milestone of
1 lakh EV sales in FY25, cementing its position as India's dominant EV manufacturer with
approximately 62% market share in the passenger EV segment. The Nexon EV and Punch EV were
the top-selling models, together accounting for over 70% of total EV volumes.

On the international front, Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) — Tata Motors' UK-based luxury subsidiary
— reported EBIT margin of 8.9% for Q3 FY25, above the company's medium-term guidance of
8%. Order book for Range Rover and Defender models remains healthy at approximately 1.6 lakh
units globally. JLR's management flagged uncertainty around potential US tariff changes on
imported vehicles, which could impact profitability if implemented.

Consolidated net debt at Tata Motors reduced to ₹14,200 crore, a significant improvement
from the ₹48,000 crore peak in FY21, driven by sustained free cash flow generation from JLR
and a disciplined capital allocation strategy.

Analysts remain broadly positive on the stock, with target prices ranging from ₹850 to ₹1,020.
Key risks include EV subsidy rationalisation in India and any deterioration in JLR order intake
due to macroeconomic weakness in the UK and EU.
