# Maruti Suzuki Launches Hybrid Variant of Brezza, Bets on Strong Hybrid Demand

**Date:** February 20, 2025
**Ticker:** MARUTI | **Sector:** Automobile

Maruti Suzuki India launched the hybrid variant of its popular Brezza SUV, expanding its
strong hybrid (SHV) lineup that already includes the Grand Vitara and Invicto. The Brezza
Hybrid is priced between ₹13.5 lakh and ₹16.2 lakh (ex-showroom), positioning it directly
against Tata Motors' Nexon in a segment where electrification is rapidly gaining traction.

Maruti has publicly chosen a hybrid-first strategy over full battery electric vehicles,
citing India's underdeveloped charging infrastructure and the total cost of ownership
advantage that hybrids offer to customers in tier-2 and tier-3 cities. The company's
management argued that hybrids deliver 35–40% better fuel efficiency versus petrol equivalents
without range anxiety.

For Q3 FY25, Maruti reported domestic volumes of 5.46 lakh units, a 5.3% year-on-year growth.
Average selling price per vehicle increased to ₹6.89 lakh, driven by favorable product mix
toward SUVs and premium variants. Operating margin stood at 11.5%, aided by easing commodity
costs particularly steel and aluminium.

The hybrid vs. EV strategic divergence between Maruti and Tata Motors makes the automobile
sector particularly interesting from a portfolio perspective — both companies are in the same
sector but are betting on different technology transitions with different risk profiles.
