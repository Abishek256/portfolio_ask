# Reliance Jio Adds 8.3 Million Subscribers in Q3, ARPU Rises on Tariff Hike Impact

**Date:** January 20, 2025
**Ticker:** RELIANCE | **Sector:** Energy / Conglomerate / Telecom

Reliance Jio added 8.3 million net subscribers in Q3 FY25, taking its total subscriber base
to 481 million, further consolidating its position as India's largest telecom operator by
both subscriber count and revenue market share. Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) rose to
₹203.3, reflecting the full-quarter impact of the tariff hike implemented in July 2024 —
the first significant industry-wide price increase in six years.

Jio's 5G rollout reached 10,000 cities and towns across India, with management claiming
95% population coverage in its 5G footprint cities. The company is now focusing on improving
5G monetisation through enterprise and fixed wireless access (FWA) products.

On the retail and O2C (Oil-to-Chemicals) side, Reliance's consolidated revenue for the
quarter was ₹2.43 lakh crore. The O2C segment faced margin pressure due to weak global
refining margins and higher crude oil feedstock costs, partially offsetting the strong
telecom performance.

The conglomerate nature of Reliance makes it particularly complex to analyse — a question
about "Reliance's outlook" could legitimately be answered differently depending on whether
the context is its telecom, retail, or energy segment. Retrieval systems must handle this
ambiguity carefully.
