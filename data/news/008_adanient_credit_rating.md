# Adani Enterprises: Credit Rating Outlook Revised, Group Debt Levels Scrutinised

**Date:** March 5, 2025
**Ticker:** ADANIENT | **Sector:** Infrastructure / Conglomerate

Moody's revised the outlook on Adani Enterprises Ltd's credit rating from "stable" to
"negative" citing concerns about elevated group-level debt, execution risks on its ambitious
capital expenditure pipeline, and governance questions that have lingered since the Hindenburg
Research report published in early 2023. The long-term issuer rating remains at Ba3 (below
investment grade by international standards), which limits the group's access to certain
categories of foreign institutional investors.

The Adani Group responded by releasing a detailed debt repayment schedule, highlighting that
group companies have prepaid approximately $2.65 billion in loans since the Hindenburg episode
and that the promoter share pledge ratio has been significantly reduced. Management argued
that operational cash flows from its airport, port, and renewable energy businesses are
sufficient to service existing debt obligations.

Adani Enterprises' stock has been volatile — swinging between ₹2,100 and ₹3,400 over the
past 12 months. FIIs reduced their stake from 18.2% to 15.7% over two consecutive quarters,
while domestic institutions slightly increased exposure.

For portfolio risk management, ADANIENT represents a high-beta, high-controversy position.
Its correlation with broader market sentiment is weaker than typical large-cap stocks, making
it a source of idiosyncratic risk that cannot be diversified away within the Indian equity space.
