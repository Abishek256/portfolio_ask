# Sun Pharma Receives USFDA Approval for Dermatology Drug, US Revenue Pipeline Strengthens

**Date:** February 14, 2025
**Ticker:** SUNPHARMA | **Sector:** Pharmaceuticals

Sun Pharmaceutical Industries received final approval from the US Food and Drug Administration
(USFDA) for its specialty dermatology drug Ilumya (tildrakizumab) in a new indication —
generalised pustular psoriasis (GPP). This approval expands the addressable market for one
of Sun Pharma's key specialty products in the United States, its largest revenue market.

The US specialty business now contributes approximately 18% of Sun Pharma's total revenue,
with branded specialty drugs carrying significantly higher margins than the company's generic
pharmaceutical business. Management has guided that specialty revenue will be the primary
growth driver over the next three years.

Sun Pharma also resolved a longstanding USFDA warning letter at its Halol facility in Gujarat,
clearing the way for fresh ANDA (Abbreviated New Drug Application) filings from this plant.
The resolution removes a major regulatory overhang that had capped the stock's valuation
multiple for several quarters.

Quarterly results for Q3 FY25 were in line with estimates — consolidated revenue grew 10.8%
year-on-year to ₹13,440 crore and EBITDA margin expanded to 27.1%. Sun Pharma is considered
a defensive holding in the portfolio due to its low beta, strong cash generation, and
USD-denominated export revenues that act as a natural hedge against rupee depreciation.
