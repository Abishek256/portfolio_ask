# Coal India Declares ₹5 Interim Dividend, Production Targets on Track for FY25

**Date:** February 28, 2025
**Ticker:** COALINDIA | **Sector:** Energy / PSU

Coal India Ltd declared an interim dividend of ₹5 per share for FY25, taking the total
dividend declared for the year to ₹21.50 per share so far. At the current market price of
approximately ₹418, this implies a dividend yield of around 5.1% on an annualised basis —
one of the highest among Nifty 50 constituents, making Coal India a favoured holding for
income-seeking investors.

Coal production for April–January FY25 stood at 602 million tonnes, tracking slightly ahead
of the full-year target of 780 million tonnes. Power sector demand remains robust, with
thermal power plants running at elevated Plant Load Factors (PLF) due to strong electricity
consumption growth across residential and industrial segments.

The government, which holds a 63.1% stake in Coal India, has historically extracted large
dividends from the PSU to meet fiscal revenue targets — a practice that limits Coal India's
ability to invest in productivity improvements and mine expansion. This government ownership
overhang is a structural constraint on the stock's valuation multiple.

Coal India is classified as a capital allocation concern among ESG-focused investors due to
its core business of fossil fuel extraction. Several global index funds have reduced or
eliminated exposure, creating a structural headwind to foreign institutional ownership.
Despite this, domestic retail and institutional investors value the stock for its dividend
consistency and low earnings volatility.
