# Titan Q3 FY25: Jewellery Segment Drives Revenue Beat, Watches Disappoint

**Date:** January 14, 2025
**Ticker:** TITAN | **Sector:** Consumer Goods

Titan Company reported Q3 FY25 revenues of ₹13,786 crore, a 25% year-on-year growth,
beating analyst estimates by approximately 4%. The outperformance was driven almost entirely
by the jewellery segment (Tanishq), which grew 26% year-on-year aided by the festive season
buying and the sharp rise in gold prices increasing the average transaction value.

The watches and wearables segment (Titan, Fastrack, Helios) grew a muted 6% year-on-year,
reflecting intensifying competition from international smartwatch brands and subdued discretionary
spending in the mid-market category. Management acknowledged that the wearables segment needs
product refresh to recapture growth momentum.

EBIT margin for Q3 came in at 11.3% — slightly below street expectations of 11.7% — due to
elevated gold procurement costs and higher store expansion related expenses. Titan opened
39 new Tanishq stores in the quarter, taking total Tanishq count to 498 stores across India.

A key risk for Titan is the high correlation between its jewellery revenue and gold prices.
Rising gold prices inflate revenue but compress operating margins because the cost of goods
sold rises faster than pricing. This makes Titan's reported growth partly a gold price effect
rather than a pure volume story — an important nuance for portfolio exposure analysis.
