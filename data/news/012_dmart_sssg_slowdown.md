# DMart Q3 FY25: Same-Store Sales Growth Slows, Quick Commerce Threat Intensifies

**Date:** January 13, 2025
**Ticker:** DMART | **Sector:** Retail

Avenue Supermarts (DMart) reported Q3 FY25 revenue of ₹15,972 crore, up 17.7% year-on-year,
but same-store sales growth (SSSG) — the metric that strips out contribution from new stores —
decelerated to 5.8%, its lowest in 12 quarters. The market reacted negatively, with the stock
declining approximately 7% in the two sessions following the results.

Management attributed the SSSG slowdown to increasing competition from quick commerce platforms
(Blinkit, Zepto, Swiggy Instamart) in urban markets, particularly Mumbai and Pune where DMart
has historically been strongest. Customers in tier-1 cities appear to be shifting staple grocery
purchases to 10-minute delivery apps for convenience, reducing footfall at physical DMart stores.

DMart's response has been to accelerate its own online ordering platform (DMart Ready) but
analysts remain skeptical — DMart Ready operates on a scheduled delivery model incompatible
with quick commerce expectations, and the company's warehouse-style physical store format
is not easily replicated as a dark store for rapid last-mile delivery.

DMart trades at approximately 85x trailing earnings — one of the highest PE multiples in Indian
retail — which prices in continued high growth. Any sustained SSSG deceleration creates
significant valuation risk. The stock's high PE makes it particularly sensitive to earnings
disappointments, and the quick commerce disruption narrative is still evolving.
