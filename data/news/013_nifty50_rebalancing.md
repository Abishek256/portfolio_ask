# Nifty 50 Semi-Annual Rebalancing: Two Stocks Replaced, Index Funds Must Adjust

**Date:** March 28, 2025
**Ticker:** UTI_NIFTY50_DIRECT (Indirectly affected) | **Sector:** Diversified Equity / Index

NSE Indices announced the semi-annual rebalancing of the Nifty 50 index effective April 1, 2025.
LTIMindtree will be replaced by Shriram Finance, and JSW Energy will replace Divi's Laboratories
in the index. The changes reflect the index committee's periodic review based on market
capitalisation, liquidity, and trading volume criteria.

For investors holding UTI Nifty 50 Index Fund or any other Nifty 50 tracking fund, this
rebalancing has direct implications. Fund managers will be required to sell their positions in
the outgoing stocks and buy into the incoming ones to maintain tracking accuracy. This forced
buying and selling activity can create short-term price pressure on affected stocks.

The tracking error — a measure of how closely the index fund mirrors its benchmark — is a key
metric to watch post-rebalancing. Well-managed index funds like UTI Nifty 50 Direct typically
maintain tracking error below 0.10% annually. Investors should compare this against the fund's
reported tracking error in the upcoming fact sheet.

Index fund investors often underestimate the impact of rebalancing on transaction costs and
potential capital gains distributions. In India, index fund rebalancing within the fund's corpus
does not trigger capital gains for the unit holder — the tax event occurs only at redemption.
This distinguishes Indian mutual fund taxation from some other jurisdictions where internal
portfolio changes can create tax liability.
