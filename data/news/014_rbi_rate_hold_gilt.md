# RBI Holds Repo Rate at 6.5% in February MPC Meeting, Gilt Funds React

**Date:** February 7, 2025
**Ticker:** SBI_GILT_DIRECT (Directly affected) | **Sector:** Government Securities / Debt

The Reserve Bank of India's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) voted 4-2 to hold the repo rate
unchanged at 6.5% in its February 2025 meeting, disappointing a segment of the market that
had expected a 25 basis point cut. The RBI revised its FY25 GDP growth estimate downward to
6.6% from 6.8%, acknowledging some moderation in economic momentum, while maintaining its
inflation forecast at 4.8% — still above the 4% target midpoint.

Governor Sanjay Malhotra struck a cautiously accommodative tone, signalling that the MPC
remains open to rate cuts if inflation continues its downward trajectory toward the 4% target.
Markets interpreted this as a signal that the rate cut cycle may begin in April 2025, subject
to incoming data.

Gilt funds reacted with a brief rally — the SBI Magnum Gilt Fund's NAV rose approximately
0.3% on the day as bond prices moved up slightly on the dovish tone. However, analysts caution
that gilt fund investors should not extrapolate this short-term movement. Duration risk remains
elevated — if rate cuts are delayed or if global yields rise sharply, gilt fund NAVs could
correct meaningfully.

For portfolio holders with SBI Magnum Gilt Fund exposure, the key consideration is investment
horizon. Gilt funds are appropriate only for investors who can hold for 3 years or more and
have a view that interest rates will fall over that period. Short-term holders are exposed to
mark-to-market losses if the rate cycle turns against them.
