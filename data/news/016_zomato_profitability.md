# Zomato Turns Consistently Profitable, Quick Commerce Blinkit Surpasses Food Delivery Growth

**Date:** February 10, 2025
**Ticker:** ZOMATO | **Sector:** Internet / Consumer Tech

Zomato reported its fourth consecutive quarter of profitability in Q3 FY25, with adjusted EBITDA
of ₹285 crore. The headline story, however, was Blinkit — the quick commerce subsidiary —
which for the first time contributed more revenue than the core food delivery business on a
quarterly basis, a structural inflection point that management described as validating their
acquisition thesis.

Blinkit's Gross Order Value grew 120% year-on-year as the platform expanded its dark store
network from 526 to 791 stores across 40+ cities. Average Order Value (AOV) for Blinkit rose
to ₹682 from ₹583 a year ago, driven by customers adding higher-value electronics, personal
care, and pet care products to their grocery orders.

The food delivery market in India is essentially a duopoly between Zomato and Swiggy, with
Zomato holding approximately 57% market share. Margins in food delivery have expanded as
the take rate from restaurants has increased to approximately 22-24%, approaching international
peers like DoorDash and Uber Eats.
