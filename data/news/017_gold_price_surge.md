# Gold Hits All-Time High of ₹89,000 per 10g as Global Uncertainty Drives Safe Haven Demand

**Date:** March 20, 2025
**Ticker:** N/A — Commodity | **Sector:** Precious Metals / Commodities

Gold prices in India hit an all-time high of ₹89,000 per 10 grams in March 2025, driven by
a combination of global factors: continued central bank buying by emerging market nations,
geopolitical uncertainty in the Middle East, and expectations of US Federal Reserve rate cuts
making dollar-denominated assets relatively less attractive.

Internationally, spot gold crossed $3,050 per troy ounce — a record high. The World Gold
Council reported that central banks globally purchased 1,045 tonnes of gold in 2024, the
third consecutive year of purchases exceeding 1,000 tonnes. The Reserve Bank of India has
been among the active buyers, adding approximately 72 tonnes to its foreign exchange reserves.

For Indian investors, gold has delivered approximately 21% returns in rupee terms over the
past 12 months, outperforming most equity categories. Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs), which
offer an additional 2.5% annual interest, have been particularly attractive and are redeemable
at market price at maturity, fully tax-free for individual investors.
