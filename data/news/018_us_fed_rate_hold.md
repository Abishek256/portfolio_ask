# US Federal Reserve Holds Rates at 5.25–5.50%, Signals Caution on Cuts

**Date:** January 31, 2025
**Ticker:** N/A — Macroeconomic | **Sector:** Global Monetary Policy

The US Federal Reserve held its benchmark interest rate unchanged at 5.25–5.50% at its January
2025 FOMC meeting, pushing back against market expectations of an imminent rate cut. Fed Chair
Jerome Powell stated that the committee needs greater confidence that inflation is moving
sustainably toward the 2% target before initiating rate reductions.

US CPI for December 2024 came in at 3.4% year-on-year, still above target, with core services
inflation proving particularly sticky. The labour market remains resilient — non-farm payrolls
added 256,000 jobs in December, well above the 160,000 consensus estimate.

The decision triggered a mild risk-off reaction in global equity markets. Indian markets saw
FII outflows of approximately ₹8,200 crore in the week following the announcement, as higher
US rates make dollar-denominated assets relatively more attractive, reducing the appeal of
emerging market equities.

The rupee depreciated marginally to 86.40 against the dollar. RBI was reported to have
intervened in the forex market to limit volatility. Analysts noted that a prolonged high-rate
environment in the US creates a headwind for Indian equities through the FII outflow channel,
though the domestic growth story remains independently strong.
