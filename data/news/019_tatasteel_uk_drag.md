# Tata Steel Q3 FY25: UK Operations Drag on Profitability, India Business Remains Strong

**Date:** February 6, 2025
**Ticker:** TATASTEEL | **Sector:** Metals & Mining

Tata Steel reported a consolidated net loss of ₹327 crore for Q3 FY25, weighed down by
continued losses at its UK steel operations. The Port Talbot plant in Wales, undergoing a
£1.25 billion restructuring with partial UK government funding, recorded an EBITDA loss of
approximately £180 million for the quarter as blast furnace operations wound down ahead of
the transition to electric arc furnace steelmaking.

India operations, however, delivered a strong quarter. Indian steel deliveries grew 11%
year-on-year to 5.49 million tonnes, with EBITDA per tonne of ₹14,250 — above the ₹13,500
guidance provided earlier. Domestic steel demand was supported by government infrastructure
spending and strong automotive sector offtake.

The stock has underperformed the Nifty Metal index by approximately 15% over the past year,
reflecting investor concern about the UK drag and the capital intensity of the restructuring.
Management maintained that Port Talbot losses will significantly reduce from FY26 once the
electric arc furnace becomes operational.

Analysts are divided — bulls point to the India business quality and long-term decarbonisation
thesis, while bears flag near-term earnings dilution from UK and execution risk on the
restructuring timeline. Tata Steel's situation illustrates how conglomerate structures can
create complex cross-subsidisation dynamics that obscure underlying business quality.
