# ITC Declares ₹7.50 Final Dividend, Demerger of Hotels Business on Track

**Date:** March 25, 2025
**Ticker:** ITC | **Sector:** FMCG / Conglomerate

ITC Ltd declared a final dividend of ₹7.50 per share for FY25, taking total dividend for the
year to ₹13.75 per share. At the prevailing market price of approximately ₹428, the dividend
yield stands at 3.2% — attractive relative to peers in the FMCG sector. The board also
confirmed that the demerger of ITC Hotels into a separately listed entity remains on schedule
for completion by June 2025.

The cigarettes business, which contributes approximately 40% of ITC's revenue but over 80%
of its profits, grew 8% year-on-year in Q3 FY25 driven by volume growth and selective price
increases. Management indicated that no further excise duty hike on cigarettes was announced
in the Union Budget 2025, providing a benign regulatory backdrop for the near term.

ITC's FMCG businesses (Aashirvaad, Sunfeast, Savlon) reported revenues of ₹5,240 crore,
growing 9.4% year-on-year, but EBIT margins remain thin at 8.2% compared to pure-play FMCG
peers like HUL (20%+). The agri-business segment was impacted by commodity price volatility
in wheat and spices.

The hotels demerger is expected to unlock value by allowing the hospitality business to be
valued independently on an EV/EBITDA multiple rather than being discounted within a
conglomerate structure. Post-demerger, ITC's remaining entity will be a more focused FMCG
and cigarettes company with a cleaner capital allocation story.
